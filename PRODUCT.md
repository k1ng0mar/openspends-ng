# OpenSpends NG — Product Context

## Product Purpose
OpenSpends NG is a **public budget transparency dashboard** that tracks Nigerian government allocation and spending across federal, state, and local government arms. It is a civic technology tool for journalists, policy analysts, researchers, and citizens to hold government accountable.

## Users
- **Journalists & Investigators** — Researching specific ministries, projects, and spending variances
- **Policy Analysts** — Analyzing budget allocation vs. actual expenditure across government tiers
- **Civic Tech Advocates** — Monitoring public procurement and project implementation progress
- **General Public** — Understanding where tax revenue is allocated and spent

## Brand
- **Name:** OpenSpends NG
- **Domain:** openspends.ng
- **Tagline:** Public budget transparency for Nigerian citizens
- **Register:** Product (dashboard/data tool), not brand/marketing
- **Design Philosophy:** Institutional Broadsheet — Neo-Brutalist Journalism
- **Tone:** Authoritative, precise, transparent, institutional. Never playful or casual.

## Anti-References
- No SaaS aesthetic (no purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no centered hero sections)
- No startup clichés (no "Elevate", "Seamless", "Next-Gen")
- No vibe-coded design — the interface must feel like a secure government database

## Strategic Principles
1. Data fidelity over visual flourish — every pixel serves the data
2. Institutional trust through restrained design — sharp corners, hairline borders, monospaced data
3. Accessibility first — WCAG AA contrast, semantic HTML, keyboard navigation
4. Public record, not product demo — static restrained motion, no micro-interactions
